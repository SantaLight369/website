Our Story

The Technology Student Association was started at South Fayette High School approximately 30 years ago to give students more opportunities in leadership, technology, and teamwork. The current faculty sponsor, Mr. Hausman, took over the club when the previous sponsor retired. The club was a lot smaller, compared to now, with 11 members from which 3 ended up competing in nationals. The club was an early success, so more and more people joined over the years.

A sense of family can be seen in our TSA club, making it unique to other clubs and competitions. For example, say you need help with your project or you're missing something, other competitors are willing to offer help rather than anticipating your failure. We all try to help each other out and learn from other members. Especially the younger students look for guidance and advice from any of the older members of the club.

Once the club got running, a few changes had to be made for the club to be more efficient. For instance, we didn't have much paperwork in the start and not much organization which led to everything being a rush and in chaos. This resulted in more paperwork being added and things like an events matrix. We have adjusted and made changes necessary to best suit the community we have now.

A goal that we have is to establish a TSA club for the middle school as well. This would help have everyone know each other better from other schools as well. Starting this younger can help gain more experience and get used to socializing with the other people. Another hope we have is to get someone from our club as a state officer. We are also always interested in growing our club. National TSA is to have 500,000 people in TSA overall.
